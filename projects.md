    <h2>Data Science and Machine Learning</h2>
 <h3>Featured Project</h3>
    <p><b>Remaining Useful Life Prediction for Bearings</b></p>

    <p><b>What am I trying to understand?</b><br>
    Can vibration signals tell us how close a bearing is to failure?
    </p>

    <p><b>What makes this challenging?</b><br>
    The data is noisy, time-dependent, and the failure patterns are not always obvious.
    </p>

    <p><b>How do I approach it?</b><br>
    Start with raw vibration data → clean and preprocess → extract meaningful features →
    train models → evaluate how well they predict degradation.
    </p>

    <p><b>What does this lead to?</b><br>
    Estimating remaining useful life (RUL) and enabling predictive maintenance decisions.
    </p>

    <h2>Computational Engineering</h2>
<h3>Featured Project</h3>
    <p><b>Bearing Thermal and Lubrication Analysis</b></p>

    <p><b>What am I trying to understand?</b><br>
    How do load, speed, and lubrication influence thermal behavior in bearings?
    </p>

    <p><b>What makes this challenging?</b><br>
    Multiple interacting physical effects—heat generation, dissipation, and lubrication dynamics.
    </p>

    <p><b>How do I approach it?</b><br>
    Define operating conditions → build a computational model → run simulations →
    analyze temperature and lubrication behavior.
    </p>

    <p><b>What does this lead to?</b><br>
    Better insight into system behavior and improved engineering design decisions.
    </p>
  </div>
